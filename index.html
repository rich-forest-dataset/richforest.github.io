A High-density Point Cloud Dataset for Fine-grained Forest Learning

Jinyuan Shao, Bosheng Li, Xiaochen Zhou, Chunxi Zhao, Jiaxing Zhang,
Xiangxi Tian, Raymond Yeh, Ayman Habib, Bedrich Benes, Songlin Fei*
1Purdue University
*Corresponding author

Abstract

The acquisition of 3D tree geometry enables a comprehensive and detailed understanding of forest structure and composition. A promising way for data-driven precision forest management is to use modern sensors to acquire high-density point clouds. However, existing high-density datasets are limited in scale and variety due to the expensive cost of data acquisition and the difficulty in annotations. We introduce RichForest, a large-scale and high-density and precision annotated point cloud dataset including 11 billion points in real-world forests with 323 trees and 30 billion points in synthetic forests with X trees. The real-world data were obtained from different forest ecosystems globally. The synthetic data are provided by our carefully designed 3D tree models and simulated forest ecosystems. We further show how the RichForest dataset can be used for two key tasks in 3D forestry research: leaf-wood separation and individual tree segmentation. We extensively benchmark the performance for these two tasks on both forest-specific algorithms and point cloud deep learning algorithms using RichForest. Experimental results reveal that existing algorithms face challenges in terms of generalizability and complexity. Finally, we discuss the potential of RichForest to advance the fine-grained understanding of forests.
